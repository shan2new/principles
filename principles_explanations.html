Code of Honor — Detailed Scrolls

Understand the spirit behind each principle

Principles I: 1–6

1. Fairness

Meaning: Impartial treatment and equal opportunity for everyone.

Example: In the workplace, ensuring all team members have an equal chance to present ideas during meetings.

2. Equity

Meaning: Tailoring support to individual needs to achieve true fairness.

Example: Providing extra training to new employees so they can compete equitably with experienced team members.

3. Justice

Meaning: Ensuring everyone gets what they deserve under consistent rules.

Example: A judge applying the law equally to all parties in a trial.

4. Integrity

Meaning: Adherence to moral principles in every situation, even when it is difficult.

Example: A person admitting their mistake at work instead of hiding it, even if it might have personal consequences.

5. Service

Meaning: Acting compassionately and working for the benefit of others.

Example: Volunteering at a local shelter to help those in need.

6. Excellence

Meaning: Consistently striving to perform at one’s best in every activity.

Example: A student dedicating extra hours to study and master a challenging subject.

Principles II: 7–12

7. Growth

Meaning: Continual personal or professional development.

Example: Taking additional courses to learn new skills or improve existing ones.

8. Courage

Meaning: Facing fear and adversity with conviction and bravery.

Example: Delivering a public speech despite experiencing stage fright.

9. Humility

Meaning: Maintaining a modest view of one’s importance and achievements.

Example: An expert giving credit to their team for a successful project.

10. Industry

Meaning: Hard work, diligence, and a constant effort to improve.

Example: An employee consistently going the extra mile to meet deadlines and achieve targets.

11. Temperance

Meaning: Exercising self‑restraint and moderation in actions and behaviors.

Example: Practicing moderation in spending habits even when money is abundant.

12. Modesty

Meaning: Allowing one’s actions and achievements to speak for themselves without boastfulness.

Example: Letting the quality of your work attract attention, rather than overtly promoting yourself.
